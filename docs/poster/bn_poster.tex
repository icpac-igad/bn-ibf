		\section{Introduction}
	\begin{itemize}
		\item Impact-based forecasting (IBF) plays a crucial role in disaster risk management by facilitating anticipatory decisions that aim to mitigate damage and loss of life from natural hazards.
		\item Central to IBF are risk matrices, which quantify probabilities or the impact magnitudes for events like floods.
		\item Yet, these matrices often overlook conditional factors, potential interventions, and the implications of various actions \cite{fenton2018risk}. Moreover, for IBF to be more effective as a tool for reasoning under uncertainty, it requires enhanced instruments to assist it \cite{geist2022reasoning}.
		\item The Bayesian Network \cite{judea2003bn}, a graphical representation of relationships among a set of variables provide a structured way to reason about uncertain phenomena using probabilities. This study explores its application in IBF.  
	\end{itemize}
